Adventuring

Short Rest 1 hour, spend 1 or more Hit Dice, Hit Die being equal to char level. Roll the die add Con modifier to it, regain that much hit points.

Long Rest 8 hours no more than 1 hour strenuous activity. Can pick new spells, regain hp. Regain any hit dice.

Falling 1d6 Bludg damage every 10 feet, to max 20d6. Land prone unless you avoid damage.

Armor AC your armor and shield determine your AC, you class gives you starting equipement, and some gold to spend.

Ability check DC = difficulty class, determine which ability applies. 5 very easy, 10 easy, 15 med, hard 20, very hard 25. To make ability check roll d20 add ability modifier. As with other d20 rolls add bonus and penalties.

Ability modifier subtract 10 from score divide by 2 round down, should be modifier.

Group Checks many situations one person will roll for whole group. Such as finding loot, int (perception), or social situations chr(intimidation), or surprise when sleeping. They can pick or the DM can and everyone lives with the result.

Advantage & Disadvantage Adv roll 2 d20 keep higher, Disadv roll 2, keep lower. If someone is both they cancel out and is as if they had neither.

Contests use abilities against one another higher roll with modifiers, bonuses and penalties wins. Ties are draws.

Proficiencies any roll, attack roll, saving throw, spell casting or spell saving difficulty, ability roll uses a proficiency modifier if character is proficient with said item, all listed in the character class section.

Vision lightly obscured just causes disadvantage on wis (perception) checks. Heavily obscured such as darkness gives the blindness condition. Torch and above light is reg. Less than torch, night candle light is lightly obscured. Darkness is heavily obscured. Many items list light range pg. 150 Players Handbook.

Lifestyle Wretched, No cost. Squalid, 1sp. Poor, 2sp. Modest 1gp. Comf 2gp. Wealthy 4gp. Aris 10+gp.

Travel, Overland map

Map is divided into squares. every few squares equals one day distance of travel they need a days ration and rest for.

Sight characters often know the lay of the land. Yet in unfamiliar territory reveal 1 tile in each direction from the party.

Break Neck Pace. characters can double the amount of movement per day if they move at a break neck pace. Every con modifier days at break neck pace characters gain a level of exhaustion.

Square types: mov amt per day.

  • Open grassland or farmland: 2.
  • Road: 3.
  • Restricted: 1. (Mountains, Dense forests, Swamp, i.e.)
  • Search area: 1. (Trying to find something in one square.)
  • Town: 3. Same as road.

Foraging finding water or food. Depending on the square types they are moving in or ending upon they can use forage instead of rations and reserves of water. Chart below gives DC wis (survival) in square type of getting forage.

Table below Square types: DC wis (survival) check finding food human/mount : DC wis check finding water. Only one person makes the check, if they fail they lose that day foraging following that person through the square.

  • Open grassland or farmland : Food, DC 14/5, h/m : Water, DC 10.
  • Farmland : (for 6sp a farmer lead/provides them forage)
  • Road : copies surrounding terrain, unless they meet travelers.
  • Forest light : Food, DC 12/5, h/m : Water, DC 5.
  • Forest heavy : Food, DC 10/5, h/m : Water, DC 5.
  • Town : (If end day on town, can spend lifestyle cost for food/drink that day)

Exhaustion and Starvation. Char needs 1 pound of food per day per str modifier. 1 gallon of water on a reg day, 2 gallons on hot day, every day without water is 2 levels of exhaustion. Half water is one level. Long rest with food and drink removes 1 level. Levels stack as exhaustion increases. Go without food 3 +con modifier days. Eating one reg day resets Exhaustion lvl. Char with neg str modifiers divide by mod, ie -2 divide by 2.

Order of combat.

  1. Determine surprise, If a character is being sneaky make Dex (Stealth) sneaking vs Wis (Perception) opposing. If a character fails this surprise test, they can't move or act on their first turn, and they can't react until that turn ends. Dumb-struck for a turn.

  2. Establish Positions. DM uses reason puts people where I wants them.

  3. Roll Initiative: Dex check each player and creature type. Ties determined by DM. Each round goes in order high to low. Each round initiative remains the same.

  4. Takes turns. They can move up their speed in distance(feet) split anyway they want, and take one action, and one bonus action. If you can't take action you can't take bonus action.

  5. Reactions are triggered actions taken upon a specific action of another character or monster. If you take a reaction you cannot take another until the start of your next turn. If the reaction interrupts someone's action, they can resume after the reaction.

  6. Simple stuff, you can do simple stuff too.

  7. Repeat, from take turns till retreat or death.

Actions in Combat

Attack. One melee or ranged attack

Cast a spell each spell has a casting time, most 1 action.

Dash is an action that doubles your current speed. Modifiers apply.

Disengage is an action to remove attacks of opportunity.

Dodge Until the start of your next turn any attack rolls are disadvantaged vs you if you can see them, and you make Dex rolls with advantage. No dodge if incapacitated or a speed of 0.

Help give advantage on next ability check if made before your next turn. Give ally an attack advantage, only one before your next turn.

Hide Dex check in attempt to hide, benefits for unseen.

Ready cause a reaction for a trigger. React to specific occurence later in the round, must be after your turn. Action/spell must only take 1 turn. If spell you are concentrating. When casting you cast first on your turn release at time of trigger.

Search, Wis(perception) or Int(investigation) check.

Use an Object, if using the object requires an action.

Improvising, look at ability scores for guidance.

Making an attack.

Choose a target. Determine modifiers. Resolve attack.

Attack roll. Roll d20 add appropriate modifiers. If total exceeds targets AC, the attack hits.

Modifiers to the roll Ability: Str for melee, Dex for ranged, finesse or thrown break rule. Some spells. Proficiency bonus, add when using weapon proficiency.

A 1 or 20 20 hit no matter modifiers, and is a critical. 1 is miss no matter modifier.

Unseen disadvantaged on attack roll against you if targeting right spot. If targeting wrong spot char just misses. If on character can't see you, you have advantage on attack rolls against it. Once you attack or miss you become seen.

Ranged Attack some items have one range, can't attack beyond it. Others have two which means if in the second range you are disadvantaged. Close range is disadvantaged at 5ft on a hostile creature who sees you and is not incapacitated.

Melee attacks attacks not ranged most within 5ft, some bigger are noted in monster description.

Opportunity attacks when moving out of melee range the one moving creates attack of opportunity for foe. Opportune attack is using their one reaction to make a melee attack against moving target, stopping their movement in the process. Disengage action causes no opportunity attack as do teleporting.

Two Weapon FightingIf holding two light weapons you can take bonus action to attack with offhand weapon. No ability modifier unless negative. Same applies to thrown weapons.

Grappling 1 attack, grapple, against only 1 size larget target. Using Str(athletics) vs same or Dex(acrobatics) foe picks which they want to use. Success causes grapple condition. To escape must use action with test above. You can drag at half speed unless foe is 2 sizes smaller.

Shove 1 attack, push foe 5ft or knock prone, on success of Str(athletics) vs same or Dex(acrobatics) foe picks which they use.

Cover multiple sources of cover provide whichever is the most: half +2ac, 3/4 +5ac, total not targetable.

Dmg & Healing

Hit points Any number to 0.

Damage rolls Damage die + ability modifier, Str +dmg melee and thrown javelins, Dex +dmg ranged and items that use the finesse property such as rapier and dagger. Spells tell you what to roll. Spell does damage to multiple roll damage for all targets.

Critical Hits Roll damage dice twice add together, add ability modifier.

Damage Types diff types all apply damage but use diff resistances, acid, bludgeoning, cold, fire, lightning and so on.

Resistance and Vulnerability resistance gives half damage against type, vulnerable doubles it. These effects are applied last after all other modifiers.

Instant death if damage drops you to zero and the amount less exceeds your maximum life you die.

Unconscious if you don't instantly die you fall unconscious. Ends whenever you gain hit points.

Death saving throw your turn on 0, d20, 10 or higher succeeds. 3 success, not consecutive you stabilize, 3 failure not consecutive you die. a 1 is 2 failures, a 20 gives you back 1 hp. If you take damage you get another failure, critical attacks are two failures.

Stabilize DC 10 Wis(Medicine). Stable no more death checks, heal 1 hp after 1d4 hours. Still unconscious.

Temp hit points act as shield, not as +max hp. If two sources don't add pick higher. At 0 they do not restore consciousness they shield.

Death

Death is not permanent. If an ally can get the corpse of the char out of combat and to a temple they can be resurrected.

Cost the Gods ask for half of the characters money.

Disability upon death the character rolls a d20 upon the below list, and gains that disability upon resurrection. Record which stat so that if ever restored they can lose the disability.

  • (1) -1 str, scar losing muscle in the an arm.
  • (2) -5 speed, scar losing muscle in leg.
  • (3) -1 dex, lost part of a hand.
  • (4) -5 speed, lost part of foot.
  • (5-6) -1 con, partial liver failure. If they lose a point of con modifier they lose a hp for each lvl they obtained.
  • (7-8) -1 int, hit too hard on the head.
  • (9-10) -1 wis, partial amnesia.
  • (11-12) -1 chr, hit with ugly stick.
  • (13-14) perm fright in presence of thing which killed it.
  • (15-20) Gets off with no effect.

Conditions

  • Blinded creature can't see, fails ability checks that require sight, attacks against creature have advantage, attacks from blinded are disadvantaged.

  • Charmed can't harm charmer, charmer has advantage on social interactions with charmed.

  • Deafened fails ability checks for hearing.

  • Frightened disadvantage on ability and attack rolls while source of fear in sight. Can't move closer to source of fear.

  • Grappled/Restrained speed 0. Attack rolls against are advantaged, attack rolls from are disadvantaged. Disadvantage on Dex saving rolls.

  • Incapacitated can't take actions or reactions. Loses dex saving throws.

  • Invisible see unseen in combat actions above.

  • Paralyzed incapacitated, can't move or speak. Fails Str and Dex saving throws. Attack rolls against have advantage. Attack rolls that hit are critical if within 5 feet.

  • Petrified creature and non-magical creatures stuff turn to stone, cease aging and increase in weight by order of 10. All effects of paralyzed, and gain resistance to all damage. Immune to poison and disease.

  • Poisoned disadvantage on attack and ability checks.

  • Prone reduced to crawl unless they stand. Have disadvantage on attack rolls. If attacker is within 5 feet gains advantage on prone char, otherwise has disadvantage.

  • Stunned same as paralyzed except no critical within 5 feet.

  • Unconscious same as paralyzed but in addition drops prone and drops what was being held.

  • Turned Wis saving throw, turned for 1 min or until takes damage. Tries to get away from character, can't move willingly within 30 feet. No reactions, can only dash away. No where to move can use dodge action.

Movement.

Movement can be broken up many ways. Deduct total move in feet from whichever move type they are using. Swimming, crawling cost 1 extra speed per foot. If the total equals their speed in a particular move type they must switch to different type or stop. They can switch to different types as long at the new type still has speed after the total move so far is deducted.

Difficult terrain, every foot costs 1 extra. Multiple things in the a space counts as difficult terrain. Another creature counts as difficult terrain. You can move through a non-hostile creatures space. If hostile you can only move through their space if they are 2 sizes smaller or bigger. If creature not 2 sizes smaller or bigger create an attack of opportunity. Can't end move in their space.

Prone, standing up from being knocked prone takes half your speed.

Long Jump move feet distance equal to Str if covered 10 feet before, if not half Str. Low obstacle, Str(athletics) 10DC to clear, else you hit it. If landing in difficult terrain, DC 10 Dex(acrobatics), succeed land on feet else prone.

High Jump If moved 10 feet before 3 + Str modifier. Standing only half that. Your arms can reach 1&1/2 your height above the jump.

Space Control in feet, not size but control: tiny 2 & 1/2, small 5, med 5, large 10, huge 10, Garg 20. Any creature can fit through a space once size smaller than it, though disadvantaged on attack and dex saving throws. Attacks on squeezed people are advantaged.

On a Grid Divide grid into 5ft. Must have at least 1 speed left to enter square. If difficult you have to have 2 ft for next square.

Mounted Combat and Underwater Combat

Mount any one size larger creature with proper anatomy. Once during your move any within 5 feet, costs half your move. Something moves mount against your will, DC10 Dex saving throw or fall off mount. Knocked prone same check. Mount knocked prone, use reaction to dismount land on feet. Don't fall prone within 5ft.

Control mount non intelligent mounts can take a dash, disengage, and dodge action, with initiative same as rider. Intelligent can do what it wants with its own initiative, attack of opportunity on either mount or rider.

Underwater if the creature has no natural swimming speed makes attack at disadvantage unless from dagger, javelin, shortsword, spear, or trident. Ranged attack disadvantaged at normal, no attack at long unless crossbow, net or thrown weapon like javelin.

Spells

Spell Casting Ability Spell save DC is usually 8 + proficiency bonus + Wis,Int or Chr bonus. Different casters use a different ability modifier. Spell attack modifier is the ability and proficiency bonus.

Spell slots characters have slots for spells which some slots are gained per level. Can fill higher slots with lower level spells, cast as if higher level spell. Need long rest to refill slots.

Cantrips no spell slot needed, cast at lvl 0.

Rituals either choose to perform spell as normal or as ritual. Rituals take 10 min or longer, no need of spell slot. Can't cast at higher level. Needs feature or on spell list.

Casting time most are 1 action. A few with your one bonus action, if bonus action no other spell except cantrip with your 1 action. Longer spells each turn you use your action casting with concentration.

Concentration moving and attacking, does not break concentration. Taking damage, Con-DC 10 or half the damgage, which ever is higher check, fail means no concentration. Killed or incapacitated means no concentration.

Range most spells list range.

Components verbal v, somatic s(gesture), material m, no components no spell.

Targets can't target what you can't see, if you try to target area pick the furthest point you can see.
